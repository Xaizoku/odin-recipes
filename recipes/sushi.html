Sushi

The Dish

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish featuring vinegared rice.

combined with raw or cooked seafood, vegetables, or other ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups short-grain Japanese rice
  • 2 cups water, plus extra for rinsing rice
  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 8 sheets nori (dry seaweed)
  • 4 cups prepared sushi rice
  • 1/2 pound imitation crab, flaked
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon wasabi paste
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce

Directions

  1. Rinse the rice in a strainer or colander until the water runs clear. Combine with water in a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook for 20 minutes. Rice should be tender and water should be absorbed. Cool until cool enough to handle.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Cook over medium heat until sugar dissolves. Cool, then stir into the cooked rice. When you pour this in, your rice is vinegar rice.
  3. Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat. Spread a thin layer of cool rice on the sheet of nori, and press into a thin layer. Leave a 1/2 inch margin at the top. Arrange a line of avocado, cucumber, and crab across the rice, 1 inch from the bottom. Lift the end of the mat, and gently roll it over the ingredients, pressing gently. Roll it forward to make a complete roll. Repeat with remaining ingredients.
  4. Cut each roll into 4 to 6 slices
